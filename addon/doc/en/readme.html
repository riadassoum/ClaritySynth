ClaritySynth

A neural, Arabic-first, multilingual speech synthesizer add-on for the NVDA screen reader.

ClaritySynth speaks Arabic with natural neural voices, automatic diacritization (tashkeel), and full tajweed-aware phonology. It also speaks English and — through downloadable voice packs — many other languages, and it switches between Arabic and non-Arabic automatically inside a single sentence. A separate, lightweight formant synthesizer is included as a tiny, always-available alternative.

ClaritySynth installs two synthesizers, which you pick from NVDA's synthesizer list (NVDA+Ctrl+S):

  • ClaritySynth Neural — the main, natural-sounding voice for everyday use.
  • ClaritySynth Formant — a tiny, robust fallback that needs no neural models. It can use NV Speech Player, a built-in engine, or eSpeak NG.

Quick start

  1. Press NVDA+Ctrl+S and choose ClaritySynth Neural.
  2. Press NVDA+Ctrl+V to open Speech settings.
  3. Leave the Primary voice engine on Mixer and pick one of the Arabic Neural speakers as your Voice. That is all you need — Arabic is diacritized and spoken automatically, and any English, numbers or symbols in the same sentence are handled for you.

Choosing your Arabic voice: the Primary voice engine

The first setting, Primary voice engine, decides how your Arabic voice is produced:

  • Mixer (default) — the built-in multi-speaker neural Arabic voice. With Mixer selected you can choose the Voice (speaker), the Primary voice variant (which acoustic model, when more than one is installed), and the Vocoder.
  • Piper — an Arabic Piper voice you have downloaded (such as Kareem). This option appears only once you have installed at least one Arabic Piper voice. With Piper selected, the Voice list becomes your installed Arabic Piper voices.

Both engines use the same tashkeel (diacritization) pipeline, so your Arabic is vocalized the same way whichever you choose. If you switch the engine while the settings window is open, close and reopen Speech settings so the Voice list refreshes.

The secondary (non-Arabic) voice

English and other non-Arabic text are spoken by the Secondary voice. A natural English voice is built in and used automatically. You can install more languages from the downloads window; each is spoken in its own language. The Secondary voice variant chooses a quality/speed tier.

Diacritization (tashkeel)

Undiacritized Arabic is vocalized automatically before it is spoken; text that already has diacritics is kept exactly as written. The Tashkeel library setting chooses which engine adds the marks:

  • Libtashkeel (default) and Rawi are always available.
  • CATT, Shakkala and Shakkelha appear once downloaded.
  • Off reads the text exactly as written.

Diacritize Arabic text (a Tools-menu tool)

Under the NVDA Tools menu, in the ClaritySynth submenu, Diacritize Arabic text (tashkeel) opens a window where you can paste Arabic of any length, pick a tashkeel library, and get the fully diacritized text back to copy — exactly as that library would read it. It works no matter which synthesizer is active, and it leaves any English, numbers and symbols untouched.

Downloading voices and models

Open the NVDA Tools menu, choose the ClaritySynth submenu, then Voice & model downloads. The window is organized into tabs:

  • Arabic voices — extra Arabic acoustic models and Arabic Piper voices (for the Piper engine).
  • Other-language voices — Piper voices for many languages. Voices marked RT streaming are the fastest; others are higher quality but slower.
  • Vocoders — vocoders for the Mixer engine.
  • Tashkeel libraries — extra diacritizers (CATT, Shakkala, Shakkelha).

Each voice downloads together with its configuration file. Press Escape to close the window. Downloaded items take effect the next time you switch to ClaritySynth, and they are stored outside the add-on folder, so updating or reinstalling ClaritySynth never deletes them. ClaritySynth also checks GitHub for new versions, shows the changelog, and can install an update for you.

The Formant synthesizer

ClaritySynth Formant is a separate, lightweight synthesizer with no neural models. Its Synthesis engine setting lets you choose — and cycle through — several formant back-ends:

  • Auto — the best available.
  • NV Speech Player — a rich formant DSP engine.
  • Built-in — a pure-Python engine that always works.
  • eSpeak NG — a compact multilingual formant synthesizer. Choose the eSpeak language (Arabic and over a hundred others) and the eSpeak variant (voice timbres, including the classic Klatt voices).

The Formant driver shares the same tashkeel libraries as the neural voice, and honors the same Rate, Rate boost, Pitch and Volume controls.

Settings reference

  • Primary voice engine — Mixer or Piper (see above).
  • Voice — the Arabic speaker (Mixer) or Arabic Piper voice (Piper).
  • Primary voice variant — acoustic model for Mixer; not used for Piper (the voice itself is the full choice).
  • Vocoder — the vocoder for the Mixer engine.
  • Secondary voice / Secondary voice variant — the non-Arabic voice and its quality/speed tier.
  • Tashkeel library — the diacritizer (see above).
  • Rate and Rate boost — speaking speed; Rate boost raises the top speed substantially. Fast speech stays clear (pitch-preserving time-compression on the neural voices).
  • Pitch and Volume — behave identically across languages.
  • Clarity — reduces neural noise for a cleaner voice.

Arabic phonology

ClaritySynth models correct tajweed, including raʼ tafkhim/tarqiq (heavy/light ر), emphatic consonants (ص ض ط ظ), pharyngeals (ح ع), uvulars (ق خ غ), hamzat al-wasl elision, tanween, and context-sensitive taa marbuta (ة). Reading by character announces Arabic letters and diacritic marks by their names.

Troubleshooting

  • The neural voice will not start. The neural engine needs the Microsoft Visual C++ Redistributable. If it is missing, ClaritySynth says so and explains how to install it; after installing it, restart NVDA.
  • The Piper engine is not offered. Download an Arabic Piper voice first (Tools → ClaritySynth → Voice & model downloads → Arabic voices); the Piper engine appears once one is installed.
  • A setting in the Formant driver seems missing. Some settings apply only to a particular engine — for example the eSpeak language and variant apply only when the eSpeak engine is selected.
